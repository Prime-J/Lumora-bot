# The Chronicle of Lumora — Origins

## I. The World Before the Rift

Long before factions, before walls, before the word *mora* was ever spoken in fear, there was only **Lumora** — a single continent cradled between two still seas, lit by twin moons and veined with luminous crystals that hummed beneath every hill. The land itself was alive. It breathed. The crystals, the **Lumoran resonance**, were not mined — they were *listened to*. Children pressed their ears to them to hear stories older than language.

The people of Lumora called themselves **Lumorians**. They did not know war. They knew planting seasons, crystal-song festivals, and the soft creatures of the wilds — small, luminous things no bigger than a cat, who nested in the resonance fields and fed on starlight. These creatures had no name then. They were simply *companions*.

## II. The Tear

No one agrees on the hour it happened. Some say the sky split at dawn; others swear it was midnight when the moons aligned for the first time in a thousand years. What is certain is that on that day, a wound opened in the fabric of Lumora — a jagged, weeping fissure above the Obsidian Plateau.

The Lumorians called it **the Primordial Rift**.

From it poured a second light — not warm like the sun, not silver like the moons, but *violet*, and *hungry*. It touched the crystals and turned them black. It touched the soft companions, and they **changed** — growing larger, fiercer, their eyes splitting into new colours, their bodies rippling with unstable energy the scholars would later name **Primordial Energy**.

The companions became **Mora**.

Some Mora remained gentle. Some turned savage overnight. Some flickered between both, tormented by the new power inside them. The old bond — the soft one — was gone. In its place came something deeper, wilder, and infinitely more dangerous: a resonance that could bond a Lumorian to a Mora for life, or tear both apart in a single breath.

## III. The First Bond, and the First War

A young herbalist named **Iyara** was the first to survive a bond with a changed Mora. She did not fight it. She *listened* — as her grandmother had taught her to listen to the crystals. The Mora, a storm-coated creature later remembered as the first **Sparko**, lay down beside her and did not leave.

Iyara's discovery spread. Some Lumorians learned to bond. Others were killed trying. And across the continent, three philosophies grew — quietly at first, then violently — about what the Rift *was*, and what Lumorians were meant to do about it.

- Some said the Rift was a wound to be healed, and the Mora were victims to be saved.
- Some said the Rift was a corruption to be purged, and the Mora were beasts that should never have existed.
- Some said the Rift was a gift — a doorway to power the old world was too timid to accept.

The arguments became sermons. The sermons became banners. The banners became blood.

Lumora's first war lasted seven years. It ended not with a victor, but with exhaustion — and with three strongholds rising from the ash.

## IV. The Three Factions

### 🌿 The Harmony Lumorians
They settled in the **Verdant Crescent**, where the old resonance still sang. They built the **Sanctuary**, an open facility where Mora roamed freely, cleansed of corruption when possible, and honoured as kin. Their creed: *humans and Mora are one, and the Rift is a wound we are sworn to heal.* They took the **Orb of Purification** as their symbol — a small, cupped light every Harmony Lumorian carries to this day.

### ⚔ The Purity Order
They built the **Citadel** on the eastern cliffs, a fortress of black stone and iron discipline. To them, the Mora were an abomination the Almighty had never intended, and the Rift was a devil's mouth that must be sealed. They did not cleanse Mora — they *studied* them, *controlled* them, *commanded* them. Their creed was written on the Citadel gates: *Order over chaos. Steel over mercy.*

### 🕶 The Rift Seekers
They descended into the Obsidian Plateau itself — the very edge of the tear — and built the **Rift Nexus** in its shadow. They did not fear the Primordial Energy; they drank it. They bonded with corrupted Mora on purpose. They built markets in hidden caverns where forbidden things were traded. Their creed was simple and unrepentant: *the Rift is a blessing. Power is the only prayer worth speaking.*

## V. The Long Quiet

For generations after the Three Strongholds rose, an uneasy peace held. Lumora became a world of rituals, hunts, markets, and submission ceremonies — a world where every child chose, by the age of sixteen, which Stronghold to call home. The **Lumoran Crystal** (LCR) became the common currency, mined carefully from the old resonance fields. **Rift Energy Orbs** (REOB) were hoarded by the Rift Seekers — the raw stuff of creation itself, volatile and sacred.

New Mora were created in the **Lumora Labs** — sealed facilities where, with enough Creation Powder and a Rift Energy Orb, a pattern of a new Mora could be birthed into the world. These new creatures — **2nd Generation Mora** — are rarer, stranger, and marked forever by who forged them.

Factions fought in small ways: skirmishes over hunting grounds, sanctioned wars declared in the open, arena duels between champions. Fortunes rose and fell. Titles were earned. Corruption spread, and cleansing rituals were performed. Walls were built around each Stronghold's vault — great shimmering barriers of crystal and ward-stone, constantly reinforced by the faithful.

And then, one day, **Kael the Riftwalker** appeared.

## VI. The Riftwalker

No one knows where Kael came from. Some say they once walked all three paths and were exiled from each. Others whisper that Kael is not a Lumorian at all, but a piece of the Rift that learned to wear a body. What is certain is this:

Kael wears a cracked porcelain half-mask — one eye teal, one eye amber. Their cloak shifts between violet and void. Their staff splits into three prongs, each crackling with a different faction's energy. They belong to no one. They profit from everyone. And when the factions grow complacent, Kael appears at the edges of their Strongholds and *smiles*.

Kael does not fight. Kael *opens doors*. For a price — always 10% of whoever claims the contract — the Riftwalker will tear a temporary rift between one Stronghold and another, and let the raiders through. The walls crumble. The vaults bleed. The honour of factions rises and falls with every tear Kael opens.

The Lumorians hate Kael. The Lumorians pay Kael anyway.

## VII. The World Today

This is the world you walk in now. Three Strongholds, three creeds, three armies of bonded Mora. Hunting grounds across eleven terrains, from the Ember Wastes to the Frostveil Tundra. A Primordial Rift that still hums overhead, still occasionally bleeds corruption into the air during **Rift Surges**. Markets, labs, arenas, sanctuaries.

And underneath it all, the old questions — the ones that started the first war, and will likely start the last:

*Is the Rift a wound, a sin, or a gift?*

*Are the Mora our kin, our enemies, or our weapons?*

*And when Kael next whispers your faction's name — will you claim the contract?*
